# My Language Portfolio


I grew up learning a lot of spoken languages in school. I was never really the best at them, but was good enough to keep up with the 6 months of basic content that schools often taught me, mainly covering introductions, numbers, and grammatical structure. The normal sort of stuff you'd expect to learn when you're being introduced to the basics of a new language. It was around year 4 when I first started playing around with Scratch and blocky code, and the idea of being able to create things simply hooked me. Throughout high school, I rarely focused during classes due to my rather severe ADHD and the inattentiveness that came with it. Instead, I found myself coding most of the time. Games, little console systems, simple tools. Anything I could think of or that grabbed my interest. It was the one thing that really kept me engaged and that I could focus on. Later in high school, after I had learnt two or so languages to a high degree, I developed a fixation on learning as many languages as possible. They just came easily to me because of the hyperactive side of my brain being able to recognise the intricate patterns between them. I found that I was very good at being able to understand systems, both physical and digital, and I just naturally would start nitpicking at them whenever I saw something that grabbed my attention. This fixation on learning new languages led me to learning C through a 10-week high school course run by the University of New South Wales, which further fostered my interest.

I am now putting together this repository in my third and final year of university studies at Edith Cowan University to further this interest, and as a way to showcase my abilities in learning new languages and applying them to their understood key applications in an industry context.

This repository will document my journey learning multiple programming languages, focusing on how different paradigms solve similar problems. Each section contains small experiments, notes, and projects demonstrating both fundamentals and deeper concepts.
